## The Landscape: Nobody Owns the Whole Stack

The health tracking market is **deeply fragmented**. Every player owns one slice:

| Category | Players | What They Own |
|----------|---------|---------------|
| Wearable data | Garmin, Oura, Whoop, Apple Health | Sleep, HRV, activity, steps |
| Blood work | InsideTracker, Function Health, Marek | Biomarkers, recommendations |
| Body composition | DexaFit, BodySpec | DEXA scans |
| Epigenetics | TruDiagnostic, Elysium | Biological age |
| Dashboard/QS | Gyroscope, Exist.io, Heads Up Health | Aggregation (poorly) |
| Nutrition | Cronometer, MyFitnessPal | Food logging |
| CGM/Metabolic | Levels, Dexcom | Glucose tracking |
| Longevity | Blueprint, Humanity | Protocol tracking |
| Precision Medicine | Wild Health | Doctor-guided |

**The gap:** Nobody connects ALL of these into one coherent picture. And that's exactly where Vitalis lives.

## Competitor Deep Dive

### 1. Apple Health
- **Strength:** Default aggregator, massive device ecosystem, free, trusted brand
- **Weakness:** Data graveyard — stores everything, surfaces nothing. No blood work. No insights. No intelligence layer. No cross-domain correlation. No lab PDF upload.
- **User frustration:** "It's a data dump, not a dashboard"
- **Pricing:** Free (device purchase required)
- **Vitalis advantage:** Apple Health is our data SOURCE, not our competitor. We import from it.

### 2. Gyroscope ($15-30/mo)
- **Strength:** Beautiful design, "health score" concept, location tracking
- **Weakness:** Jacked up pricing repeatedly, removed features, became AI-gimmicky. No blood work integration. No lab upload. Buggy syncs. Users are actively leaving.
- **User frustration:** "They led the game. But they started charging more for less." "Added bunch of AI stuff and increased subscription price dramatically"
- **Vitalis advantage:** We're what Gyroscope should have been — but with blood work, DEXA, epigenetics, and actually reliable syncing.

### 3. InsideTracker ($249-899/test)
- **Strength:** Best blood work analysis on market. Actionable recommendations. Allows uploading existing labs. Peer-reviewed science backing.
- **Weakness:** Expensive per test. Closed ecosystem (only their labs get full analysis). Limited wearable integration. No DEXA/epigenetic integration. App is functional but not beautiful.
- **User frustration:** "Can't upload old tests easily" "Analysis is limited for uploaded labs vs their own draws"
- **Vitalis advantage:** We're the dashboard layer InsideTracker doesn't have. Import InsideTracker data AND your own PDFs. Cross-correlate with wearable data they can't.

### 4. Function Health ($499/yr)
- **Strength:** Most comprehensive blood panel (100+ biomarkers). Clean UI. Simple pricing.
- **Weakness:** Closed system — can't upload external labs. Can't import old results. No wearable integration. No DEXA. No epigenetics. US-only.
- **User frustration:** "Not being able to upload old tests is a dealbreaker" "What do I do with this data outside their system?"
- **Vitalis advantage:** We store ALL your labs — Function, Quest, Labcorp, international — with full history. Function is a data source for us.

### 5. Whoop ($30/mo)
- **Strength:** Recovery/strain model is compelling. Community features. Journal feature for correlations.
- **Weakness:** Subscription model with no data ownership. No blood work. Limited outside of their wearable. Walled garden.
- **User frustration:** "I want my data to live somewhere I control"
- **Vitalis advantage:** Import Whoop data alongside everything else. We're the long-term home.

### 6. Oura ($6/mo + $299 ring)
- **Strength:** Best sleep tracking hardware. Readiness score. Clean app.
- **Weakness:** Limited to ring data only. No blood work. No body comp. No cross-device comparison. Subscription for features that used to be free.
- **User frustration:** "Charging monthly for features I already paid for with the ring"
- **Vitalis advantage:** Import Oura sleep data into the full picture. Correlate sleep with blood markers, body comp, training.

### 7. Levels (CGM, $199/mo)
- **Strength:** Metabolic health focus. Real-time glucose data. Good educational content.
- **Weakness:** Extremely expensive. Single metric focus. Limited long-term value after the learning period.
- **Vitalis advantage:** Import glucose data as one signal among many.

### 8. Blueprint (Bryan Johnson)
- **Strength:** Comprehensive protocol, extreme longevity focus, biological age tracking
- **Weakness:** One person's protocol, not personalized. Not really an app — it's a brand.
- **Vitalis advantage:** We track what Blueprint measures, but personalized to YOU.

### 9. Heads Up Health
- **Strength:** Actually tries to aggregate labs + wearables. Manual entry. Export.
- **Weakness:** Ugly. Clunky. Small team. Limited parser support. Feels like 2015.
- **Vitalis advantage:** Same concept, 10x better execution. Modern design. AI-powered parsing. Automated syncing.

## Top 5 Unmet Needs (What Nobody Does Well)

### 1. Cross-Domain Intelligence
No app connects: "Your HRV dropped 20% this week → your testosterone was low last draw → you've been sleeping 6 hours → here's what to do."
Everyone shows numbers. Nobody connects the dots across wearable + blood + body comp + lifestyle.

### 2. Universal Lab Upload That Actually Works
InsideTracker lets you upload, but analysis is limited. Function won't take external labs. Most apps can't parse a Quest PDF at all. Users are stuck manually entering 50+ biomarkers.

### 3. Long-Term Data Ownership & Portability
Wearable companies die. Apps pivot. Subscriptions lapse. Users lose years of health data. Nobody guarantees: "Your data lives here forever, exportable anytime, in standard formats."

### 4. Family/Household Health Tracking
Couples and families track health together but have zero shared infrastructure. No "family dashboard" exists that lets a wife and husband see trends together while maintaining individual privacy.

### 5. Beautiful, Non-Clinical UI for Serious Health Data
Gyroscope was the closest but degraded. Everything else looks like a hospital EMR or a spreadsheet. Nobody has nailed: "premium wellness aesthetic + serious data depth."

## Top 5 Things Users Hate About Existing Apps

1. **Walled gardens** — "My Oura data can't talk to my blood work"
2. **Price creep** — "They keep raising prices and removing features" (Gyroscope, Whoop, Oura)
3. **Can't upload existing data** — "I have 3 years of Quest labs I can't import anywhere"
4. **Dumb dashboards** — "It shows me numbers but doesn't tell me what they mean together"
5. **Data loss anxiety** — "If this company dies, I lose everything"
